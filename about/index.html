Education and Background

Mike Zara earned his Juris Doctor (J.D.) from the University of Denver Sturm College of Law, one of the top law schools in the Rocky Mountain region. He completed his undergraduate studies at Arizona State University. His legal education provided a strong foundation in trial advocacy, civil litigation, and tort law that he has applied throughout his career.

Personal Injury Practice

Attorney Zara handles a comprehensive range of personal injury cases, including car accidents, truck accidents, motorcycle accidents, slip and fall injuries, wrongful death claims, medical malpractice, dog bite injuries, and workplace injury cases. He is committed to providing aggressive, personalized representation to every client.

Mike understands that insurance companies are not looking out for injured people. Their goal is to minimize payouts and protect their bottom line. That is why he takes the time to thoroughly investigate every case, calculate the full extent of damages, and fight for every dollar his clients deserve.

Multi-State Practice

One of the unique strengths of Zara Injury Law is Mike's ability to represent clients in six states: Colorado, Arizona, Florida, Georgia, North Carolina, and Wisconsin. This multi-state practice allows him to serve clients across a wide geographic area and brings a breadth of legal knowledge that benefits every case.

Business Law Practice

In addition to personal injury, Mike Zara also maintains a business law practice focused on helping entrepreneurs, startups, and small businesses with entity formation, contracts, compliance, and general counsel services. This dual practice gives him a well-rounded understanding of both individual and commercial legal needs.

Philosophy

Mike believes that everyone deserves access to quality legal representation, regardless of their financial situation. That is why Zara Injury Law operates on a contingency fee basis for personal injury cases. There is no fee unless Mike wins your case. He offers free consultations to evaluate potential claims and provide honest assessments of each case's strengths and challenges.
