Georgia Personal Injury Law

Georgia follows modified comparative negligence under O.C.G.A. 51-12-33. If you are 50% or more at fault, you cannot recover. Georgia does not cap compensatory damages but caps punitive damages at $350,000 in most cases, with exceptions for DUI and intentional misconduct.

Key Georgia Injury Law Facts

  • Statute of Limitations: 2 years (O.C.G.A. 9-3-33)
  • Fault System: Modified comparative fault (50% bar)
  • Damage Caps: No cap on compensatory damages; $350K cap on punitive damages per O.C.G.A. 51-12-5.1
  • Insurance System: Tort state (at-fault system)
